Read My Bio

Hi everybody! My name is Max and I live in Tucson, AZ. I am an Arizona native and grew up in the eastern part of the state. In my free time I like to watch baseball and follow the Boston Red Sox, visit art museums, as well as hike, swim, and spend time with my nephews and nieces.

I completed studying full stack web development in July, 2019 and I love every minute of it. No longer do I dread reading graduate-level theoretical text. Rather, I am eager to find free time to work on applications to learn web development. They say it's not work if you love what you do!

I enjoy working on enterprise software and educational technology. From 2012 - 2019, I led the charge for the SALT Center Application on campus. I was responsible for troubleshooting and managing the technical functioning of the application. The application is a portal for new students and the new student enrollment record. Each year, I evaluated the application and administrative components.

I determined enhancement needs to streamline the student-facing experience and the application review business process. I began the process with thoughtful and student-focused analysis with the Admissions team. I compiled the recommendations made during discussions into Help Desk Tickets and then collaborated with SASG for implementation, testing, and deployment. My application enhancement efforts contributed to a 21% new student matriculation increase from Fall 2013 to Fall 2017.

I am an alumnus of the University of Arizona with a B.A. in Art History. I graduated from Northern Arizona University in 2016 with my Master's Degree in Educational Leadership, CCHE (Community College and Higher Education).
